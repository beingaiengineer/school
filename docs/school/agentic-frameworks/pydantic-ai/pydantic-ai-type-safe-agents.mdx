## 2. Engineering Story

A fintech startup's LLM-powered advisory agent was returning financial recommendations as raw strings. They parsed them with regex — `re.search(r"invest \$(\d+)", response)` — and it worked until the model phrased things differently. "Put $500 into ETFs" matched. "I'd recommend a $500 ETF allocation" didn't. Silent failures, wrong advice going to users, and one angry compliance officer.

They migrated to PydanticAI and defined a typed `Agent[None, InvestmentAdvice]` where `InvestmentAdvice` was a strict Pydantic model with `amount: Decimal`, `asset_class: Literal["ETF", "Bond", "Stock"]`, and `risk_level: int`. Now mypy caught type mismatches at development time, not in production. If the model returned an invalid asset class, PydanticAI rejected it before it ever touched the business logic. The compliance officer stopped getting angry. Type-safe agents aren't about bureaucracy — they're about catching the model's inconsistency at the boundary before it becomes a business problem.
