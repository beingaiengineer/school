## 2. Engineering Story

A top-performing senior engineer at a SaaS company was promoted to Engineering Manager. For the first three months, they kept writing code, fixing bugs, and trying to be the best developer on the team. The result? The team missed their quarterly goals, two engineers quit due to lack of career growth, and the new EM was severely burnt out. The wake-up call happened during a 1-on-1 with their Director. The EM learned that their output was no longer measured by lines of code, but by the output of their team. They stopped coding, started unblocking their engineers, managing stakeholder expectations, and having difficult conversations about performance. They realized that an EM's job is not to build the product, but to build the team that builds the product.
