## 2. Engineering Story

During the peak of the pandemic, a major food delivery app's engineering team was burning out trying to build five different features requested by various stakeholders. The turning point came when a ruthless Product Manager stepped in. Instead of passing requirements to the engineers, the PM looked at the data and killed four of the features. They realized that users didn't want social sharing or advanced filters; they just wanted a 'no-contact delivery' toggle. The PM shielded the engineering team from executive noise, defined a clear, minimal scope, and the team shipped the critical feature in three days. A great PM doesn't just give engineers work; they ruthlessly prioritize what *not* to build so the team can focus on what actually moves the needle.
