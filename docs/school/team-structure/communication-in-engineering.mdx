## 2. Engineering Story

A globally distributed remote startup was drowning in Zoom fatigue. Engineers in India were staying up until 2 AM to attend design meetings with their US counterparts. Velocity dropped because developers spent half their day in sync meetings instead of writing code. The VP of Engineering enforced a strict "Async-First" policy. Before any meeting could be scheduled, an engineer had to write a 1-pager (an RFC or Design Doc) and share it on the internal wiki. The team was given 48 hours to leave comments. To everyone's surprise, 80% of proposed meetings were cancelled because the decisions were resolved entirely in the document's comment thread. They learned that writing forces clear thinking, and async communication respects the maker's schedule, separating the great engineering cultures from the chaotic ones.
