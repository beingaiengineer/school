## 2. Engineering Story

In 2002, Amazon's CEO issued a famous mandate that completely changed how their engineering teams collaborated. He decreed that all teams must expose their data and functionality through service interfaces, and teams must communicate with each other exclusively through these APIs. Before this, teams relied on ad-hoc emails, shared databases, and messy internal libraries, leading to massive friction whenever two teams needed to launch a joint feature. By forcing API-driven collaboration, teams became decoupled. If the checkout team needed data from the inventory team, they just called the API instead of scheduling three meetings. This shift from human-to-human coordination to system-to-system contracts is the secret to how massive tech companies ship software at scale without collapsing under communication overhead.
