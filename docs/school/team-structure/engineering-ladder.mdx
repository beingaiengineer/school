## 2. Engineering Story

At a mid-sized e-commerce company, a talented IC (Individual Contributor) threatened to quit because the only way to get a pay raise was to become a manager, a job they had zero interest in doing. Recognizing they were about to lose their best architect, the company overhauled their career tracks. They introduced a dual-track Engineering Ladder. Now, an engineer could progress from L3 (Junior) to L4 (Mid) to L5 (Senior). After L5, they could choose the Management track (Engineering Manager, Director) OR the Technical track (Staff Engineer, Principal Engineer). Both tracks offered equal pay and prestige. This saved the company from the classic "Peter Principle"—promoting great engineers into terrible managers—and proved that a transparent, well-defined career ladder is the ultimate retention tool.
