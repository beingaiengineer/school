## 2. Engineering Story

As a ride-sharing unicorn scaled from 50 to 500 engineers, their single monolithic codebase and flat team structure collapsed. Every deployment caused merge conflicts, and no one knew who owned the notification service. To survive, they adopted a modular structure inspired by the "Spotify Model". They broke the massive engineering org into small, autonomous "Squads" of 6-8 engineers, each owning a specific microservice (like payments or routing). These squads were grouped into larger "Tribes". While this solved the deployment bottlenecks, it created a new problem: frontend engineers across different squads were using five different UI libraries. They had to introduce "Chapters" (horizontal guilds) where all frontend engineers met bi-weekly to align on a single component library. This evolution proved that your org structure dictates your software architecture.
