## 2. Engineering Story

When a massive cloud infrastructure company needed to migrate thousands of databases to a new architecture with zero downtime, the task fell to a Staff Engineer. Unlike the Tech Lead, who was busy managing the day-to-day sprint velocity and technical decisions of a single 8-person team, the Staff Engineer didn't have a specific team. Instead, they spent weeks writing a comprehensive Request for Comment (RFC) document, aligning five different engineering teams, and prototyping the migration script. The Tech Lead focused on the *tactics* of how their specific team would execute their portion of the work, while the Staff Engineer focused on the *strategy* and cross-team alignment. This migration highlighted the critical difference: Tech Leads drive team execution, while Staff Engineers solve ambiguous, cross-organizational technical problems.
