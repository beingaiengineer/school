## 2. Engineering Story

When a major tech firm went fully remote, their productivity initially plummeted. Engineers felt isolated, PRs sat unreviewed for days, and juniors were stuck without the ability to tap a senior on the shoulder. The turnaround happened when the team embraced "working in the open." They replaced private Slack DMs with public channel threads so anyone could search for solutions. They implemented strict SLA guidelines for PR reviews (must be reviewed within 24 hours), and seniors started hosting open "office hours" on continuous audio channels where juniors could drop in. They realized that you can't just copy the office environment to Zoom; successful remote engineering requires deliberate over-communication, extreme documentation, and a culture of high trust and high autonomy.
