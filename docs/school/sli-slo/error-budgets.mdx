## 2. Engineering Story

In software engineering, balancing feature velocity with system reliability is notoriously difficult. Google's SRE teams solved this by enforcing an 'Error Budget Freeze.' An error budget is the allowed threshold of failures (e.g., 0.1% of requests per month). When a product team pushes too many buggy features and exhausts their error budget, the CI/CD deployment pipeline is literally locked by the SRE team. The product team must halt all new feature launches and focus 100% of their engineering capacity on reliability, testing, and technical debt until the budget recovers in the next rolling window. This data-driven policy permanently ends the argument between product managers wanting speed and operations teams wanting stability.
