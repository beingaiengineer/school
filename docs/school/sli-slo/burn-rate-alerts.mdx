## 2. Engineering Story

Alert fatigue is a massive problem in Site Reliability Engineering. A major audio streaming service previously used static threshold alerts, causing engineers to get paged at 3:00 AM for minor 2-minute latency spikes that resolved themselves. To fix this, they implemented 'multi-window, multi-burn-rate' alerting based on Prometheus. Instead of alerting on a single spike, the system calculates how fast the error budget is being consumed (the burn rate). If an issue is consuming the 30-day budget at a rate that would deplete it in 3 days, a high-urgency page is fired. If it's a slow burn, an email or Slack ticket is created for business hours. This mathematically eliminated false-positive pages while ensuring critical outages were still caught instantly.
