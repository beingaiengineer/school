## 2. Engineering Story

A junior engineer configured an alert for 'error rate > 5% for 5 minutes'. It triggered constantly on low-traffic nights when a single error skewed the percentage, causing severe alert fatigue. They switched to burn rate alerts to only page the on-call engineer when the error budget was rapidly depleting, saving their sleep.

## 3. Real-World Analogy

Your phone battery dropping 1% an hour is normal. Dropping 1% every minute is an emergency that needs immediate attention before the phone dies completely. Burn rate measures that speed.

## 4. Concept Explanation

Burn rate measures how fast you are consuming your error budget relative to the time window. Alerting on burn rate rather than simple thresholds drastically reduces alert fatigue. A high burn rate (e.g., 10x) means you will exhaust your budget quickly and need to page someone immediately. A low burn rate (e.g., 1.1x) might just generate a ticket.

## 5. Syntax Table

| Term | Definition | Formula |
|---|---|---|
| Burn Rate | Speed of budget consumption | Actual Error Rate / Budgeted Error Rate |
| 1x Burn | Consuming budget exactly on pace | Exhausts budget at end of window |
| 10x Burn | Consuming budget 10 times faster | Exhausts budget in 1/10th the window |

## 7. Real-World Engineering Example

```yaml
# Prometheus Multi-Window Burn Rate Alert
- alert: HighErrorRate
  expr: |
    (
      job:slo_errors_per_request:ratio_rate1h{job="my_service"} > (14.4 * 0.001)
    and
      job:slo_errors_per_request:ratio_rate5m{job="my_service"} > (14.4 * 0.001)
    )
  labels:
    severity: page
  annotations:
    summary: High error budget burn rate
```

## 10. Top Interview Questions

| Question | Answer |
|---|---|
| Why use burn rate over static thresholds? | It prevents noisy alerts during low traffic and catches slow degradations. |
| What is a multi-window burn rate alert? | Checking both a short and long time window to ensure the issue is persistent. |
| What does a 1x burn rate mean? | You are on track to use exactly 100% of your budget by the end of the window. |
| What should you do for a 10x burn rate? | Page the on-call engineer immediately. |
| How do burn rate alerts reduce alert fatigue? | By only alerting when the overall SLO is actually threatened. |

## 11. Tricky Questions & Edge Cases

Can you have a high burn rate but still be within your overall error budget? Yes, a sudden spike causes a high burn rate, alerting you before the budget is completely gone.

## 12. Real-World Usage

Google SRE uses multi-window, multi-burn-rate alerting as their standard practice to minimize page fatigue.

## 13. Best Practices

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| Use multiple burn rate thresholds | Don't rely on a single alert threshold |
| Use multi-window checks (e.g. 5m and 1h) | Don't page for a 1-minute spike |

## 14. Production Notes

## 15. Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Correction |
|---|---|
| Paging on 1x burn rate | 1x burn rate is normal; only page on high rates (e.g., 10x) |
| Short evaluation windows | Use longer windows to smooth out data |
