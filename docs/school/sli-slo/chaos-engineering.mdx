## 2. Engineering Story

Netflix revolutionized cloud reliability with a philosophy: 'The best way to avoid failure is to fail constantly.' They created a tool called Chaos Monkey that randomly terminates EC2 instances in production during business hours. Instead of waiting for a server to unexpectedly crash at 3:00 AM on a Sunday, Chaos Monkey forces developers to experience failure while they are awake and at their desks. This 'shift-left' injection of pain incentivized engineers to build stateless, redundant, and highly fault-tolerant microservices. When a massive AWS US-East-1 region outage occurred in 2015, Netflix survived with minimal impact because their systems had already been tested by chaos thousands of times.
