## 2. Engineering Story

In the early days of cloud computing, service providers offered abstract '99.9% uptime' guarantees without defining what 'uptime' actually meant to the end user. Google revolutionized this by introducing precise Service Level Indicators (SLIs) - like the HTTP 500 error rate of a specific checkout API. They then set a Service Level Objective (SLO) that this specific indicator would remain successful 99.9% of the time. This clear mathematical boundary formed the basis of their Service Level Agreements (SLAs) with enterprise clients. By shifting from abstract server metrics to user-centric SLIs, engineering teams could finally measure reliability exactly as the customer experiences it, paying out SLA credits only when true user pain was measured.
